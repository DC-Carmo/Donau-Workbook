Lineout Fundamentals

The Foundation of the Source

In elite play, "The Source" is non-negotiable. Mastery of all four pillars is the prerequisite for all tactical systems.

T

Throwing

  • To Target — Precision delivery to a specific jumper at predetermined peak
  • To Space — Tactical delivery into an open corridor; timing over static target
J

Jumping

  • Split — Explosive footwork for immediate separation from marker
  • Front Foot — Maximizes vertical acceleration from lead foot
  • Back Foot — Adjusts to deep or varying trajectories
L

Lifting

  • Vertical Line — Straight-up-and-down CoG. Any deviation compromises jumper
  • Front Lifter — Initial hoist and forward stability
  • Back Lifter — Primary engine; drives to peak height
D

Delivery

  • Off the Top — Immediate transfer to scrum-half at apex
  • Down and Pop / Drive — Ball to ground for pop runner or maul anchor
  • Shift — Redirect point of contact on landing to exploit gaps

SPARK Identification System

A prescriptive system designed for reliability under high-pressure environments. Five principles govern all lineout decisions.

1

Win the Ball

The non-negotiable objective. All other decisions are secondary.

2

SET — Same Every Time

Total process consistency to ensure flawless execution under fatigue and pressure.

3

Weekly Iteration

One New Item rule per week to maintain technical clarity and avoid overload.

4

Numbers Low on Own Goal Line

Prioritize secure exit strategies. Minimize numbers near our own line.

5

04

Defensive Systems

Concepts & Frameworks

Three non-negotiable principles and three structural responses govern every defensive lineout.

Three Pillars of Defense

Air Pressure

Bodies in the air to challenge every throw and deny clean possession at source.

Hedge Your Bets

Split focus between two options at 50% — never commit 100% to one and leave the other vacant.

Disruption / Steal

Aggressive targeting of the ball to force turnovers or messy deliveries. Highest reward, highest risk.

System Definitions

MIR

Mirror

"Nose-to-nose" reactivity. Match the opposition's movement step-for-step.

POD

Pod

Pre-defined jumping blocks to shut down specific zones regardless of opposition movement.

HNG

Hinge

Reactive pivot on a fixed point (front or middle) to adjust jump block to "long" or "short" movement.

05

Spears & Nets

Maul Defense · 5m–15m Zone Only

Specialized structure employed strictly between the 5m and 15m lines. Defenders distributed horizontally to create a defensive wedge targeting every maul seam.

Zone restriction: Spears & Nets applies only between 5m and 15m lines. Outside this corridor, revert to standard defensive lineout structures.

Spears

Props (P) + Jumpers (J) — Central Core

Objective: Penetration. Drive into the maul seam, splitting the attacking formation at contact to kill forward momentum.

Nets

Hooker (2) + Flanker (7) — Flanks

Objective: Containment. Wrap the edges to catch horizontal leakage or breakout runners. Prevents the maul from being spun wide.

Attacking Maul

Principles of the Feared Maul

An effective maul is a psychological weapon. Three non-negotiable physical attributes define a "Feared" maul.

Narrow

Maximum tightness to deny defensive "swimmers" access to the ball carrier. Zero gaps in the pod.

Low

Win the leverage battle by keeping collective CoG below the defense's hands.

Long

Extend maul depth to keep the ball as far from defensive Spears as possible. Distance = time.

Tactical objective: A constant scoring threat that forces the defense to over-commit personnel — creating space for the backs or driving directly to the line. A maul that does not threaten the line is not feared.

07

Session Implementation

Practical Coaching Task Structure

Session delivery must follow this rigid three-step progression. Each step builds on the last — do not skip or reorder.

1

Fundamental Activity

Drills isolated on the Vertical Line — lifting, throw-to-space timing, and the three jumping footwork patterns (Split / Front Foot / Back Foot).

2

Source Activity

Implementation of 4/5-man attacking systems or defensive Mirror / Pod / Hinge responses. Focus on the "Call = 50" communication loop.

3

Maul Activity

Application of Spears & Nets roles (P/J as Spears, 2/7 as Nets) or attacking maul "Narrow, Low, Long" discipline.

Coaching Note: Rotate between Coaching Lead (execution) and Observer (technical correction / audit) roles throughout each activity block.
