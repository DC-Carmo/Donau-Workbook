LO Maul Attack
Roles & Responsibilities
🎯
Target
The Jumper

Triple extension on landing. Low and spread feet. Establish the anchor of the maul.

↑
Pointers
The Lifters

Slightly turn on the way down. Stay engaged, protect and attack forward — Point / Spear.

🏉
Ballers
+1 or first receiver

Gets the ball — set the height right to receive cleanly and distribute.

🔩
Screws
Shoulders to Bums

Protect the Baller. Shoulder on to bums or into gaps to create distance from opposition.

⚙️
Backers
Fill space first

Effect change. Anticipate changes of direction, bodies and breakaway.

★ Important Updates / Small Details — Big Impact

Attacking Maul — Screws closer to the touchline head inside, further from the touchline head out.
* Hooker becomes a Screw when throwing to the front of the LO instead of a Backer, for speed and safety.

F
Feet
Stance Width

Just outside shoulder width apart. If split stance, in line with the body in a stable position.

Ground Contact — Critical

Ensure contact with the ground is maintained throughout the throw — especially when releasing the ball. Loss of ground contact kills accuracy and power.

Power Generation

To increase power, "Rock Back" and come up on to toes if needed. Load the back leg, drive through the throw.

Key Cue

Feel the ground. Stay connected from start to finish.

C
Core
Engage Large Muscle Groups

Engage large muscle groups to ensure stability and generate power. The throw starts from the floor, through the core.

Core & Glutes — Switched On

Core and glutes must be "Switched On" throughout the entire duration of the throw. Not just at the start — every single phase.

STABILITY
Core braced throughout
POWER
Glutes drive the throw
G
Grip
Dominant Hand

Place fingers of dominant hand along the seam of the ball. This is your control hand — precision starts here.

Non-Dominant Hand

Ensure non-dominant hand has a firm grip of ball, fingers spread. This is your guide hand — it controls the trajectory.

Create Flight & Spin
"Roll"

Roll the ball with fingers of dominant hand to create spiral spin.

"Flick"

Flick the ball with thumb of non-dominant hand to direct flight.

C&A
Chest & Arms
"Proud Chest"

Maintain a proud, open chest throughout the entire throw. Collapsing the chest kills power and accuracy.

Load Position

Bring the ball behind head, with arms in a comfortable position, elbows tight. This is your power position — load it properly.

"Finish Strong"

Chest up, arms pointing at the target on release. You throw where your arms finish — point at the jumper's hands.

Key Cue

Proud chest in. Load up. Finish strong — arms at the target.

Full Checklist — Pre-Throw Routine
1
Step 1 — Feet

Set your width. Check your balance. Feel the ground. Decide split or parallel.

2
Step 2 — Core

Switch on core and glutes. Brace. Create a stable platform from the ground up.

3
Step 3 — Grip

Dominant hand on seam. Non-dominant firm with fingers spread. Set your release point.

4
Step 4 — Chest & Arms

Proud chest. Load behind head. Elbows tight. Finish strong — arms at the target.

The Golden Rule

Use the checklist. Same set up every time. Consistency beats talent. Process beats pressure.
