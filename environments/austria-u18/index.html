Game Model

Selection Standard

The game model is the shared picture the squad is selected to execute. It defines how Austria should look under pressure, not just what to call.

National Identity

  • Every action must look like an Austria player, not an individual solving the game alone.
  • Arrive camp-ready: language, role, and expectation already understood.
  • Unit connection matters more than isolated effort.
  • Pressure the game with intent, discipline, and repeatability.
  • Hold composure when momentum swings or the game goes off-script.

Attack Principles

  • Play flat enough to threaten, calm enough to stay connected.
  • Reload faster than the fold.
  • See the field picture before the ball leaves the source.
  • Forwards and back three solve space together.
  • Finish pressure with clear decisions, not hopeful ones.

Defensive DNA

  • Dominant line speed, every man, every phase.
  • Inside connection gives the outside line permission to hunt.
  • Win first contact, then reload with discipline.
  • Talk early. Fix edges before the ball gets there.
  • Turn pressure into territory, then back it up with repeat effort.

Program Introduction
Austria U18 National Team

This environment is built to reduce noise. The squad is selected to execute a clear model, not to learn a new language every week.

Selection earns the jersey. Daily behaviour earns trust inside the jersey.

The program expects players to bring their strengths from domestic environments into a national framework that is fast to install, competition-ready, and connected under pressure.

National Identity
Represent · Compete · Finish

Represent means every action reflects the standard of wearing Austria on the chest.

Compete means contests are attacked early: set piece, collision, kick chase, and fold.

Finish means pressure is completed with detail, discipline, and calm late in the game.

The jersey should feel heavier than the moment, never lighter.
